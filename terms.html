3. How the Send Anonymously Flow Works

The Send Anonymously flow is designed to be simple and explicit:

  1. Initiate Send: The Sender opens the Send page, provides a recipient email, picks an amount and receives a serial code.
  2. Summary: The Sender reviews a transaction summary (recipient email, amount in USD, serial code). The Sender must check a terms checkbox and agree before proceeding.
  3. Payment: The Sender is redirected to a payment processor (e.g., Paystack) to complete the payment using card. We may convert USD to NGN for settlement if required by the processor.
  4. Confirmation & Storage: If the payment completes successfully, the Service records the transaction in the Sender's transaction history. The transaction status becomes: Transaction Successful. Ready to be Claimed.
  5. Claiming Funds: The Recipient may claim the funds by presenting the serial code or following the claim flow. The amount available to claim is the Sent amount less the Service fee described below (11.8%).

The Service records the transaction metadata (recipient email, serial, date/time, transaction amount, status, and payment processor reference) in the user's transaction history. See the “Transaction Storage” section below for details on retention and access.

Important behaviour note

If the payment attempt fails at the payment processor (for example due to currency or card issues), the transaction remains pending; you may retry or cancel. Failed payment attempts are not considered completed transactions and will not be marked as "Ready to be Claimed."

4. Fees & Charges (How We Charge 11.8%)

LegalTenderPay charges a service fee of 11.8% (eleven point eight percent) on every Send transaction. This fee is included in the total amount the Sender pays, and is deducted from the funds the Recipient receives when claiming.

Put simply: if a Sender submits an amount, that amount is treated as the gross amount for the purpose of the transaction (the amount displayed and confirmed by the Sender). When the Recipient claims, the net amount the Recipient receives equals:

Recipient amount = Sent amount − (Sent amount × 11.8%)

The Service will always show the fee on the transaction confirmation and in transaction details so both Sender and Recipient can see how the fee was applied.

Why include the fee inside the total? This design means the Sender picks the face value (for example $100). The Recipient will receive that value less the platform fee. The Sender pays the full $100 to cover the intended transfer as shown in the UI.

The Service may also pass on any applicable payment processor fees or taxes to the Sender at our discretion, in a clear manner at checkout. If payment processor fees are applied extra to the Send total they will appear on the payment screen before final approval.

12.7 Q: How is the 11.8% fee applied technically?

A: When a Sender initiates a Send of amount A, the system stores the amount A as the gross amount. The fee F = A × 0.118 is computed and recorded. The Recipient amount R = A − F is computed and recorded as the net payable on claim. Both F and R are stored with the transaction record and shown in the UI.

13. Fee Calculation Details

This section shows calculations and internal rules for clarity.

  1. Input: Sender enters gross amount A (for example $100.00).
  2. Compute: Fee F = roundToTwoDecimals(A × 0.118).
  3. Net to recipient R = roundToTwoDecimals(A − F).
  4. Store: Record A, F, R, and the rounding adjustments in the transaction metadata for transparency.

Rounding policy: we round monetary values to two decimals (USD cents). Example: if the result of a multiplication yields more than 2 decimal places, we round to the nearest cent using standard round half away from zero rules.

Worked arithmetic example (detailed)

Sender amount A = $123.45
Fee F = $123.45 × 0.118 = $14.5561 → rounded to $14.56
Recipient amount R = $123.45 − $14.56 = $108.89

We record the raw computed fee and the rounded fee in the transaction object so the audit trail is explicit.
